1.5.3
12 balls numbered 1 through 12 are placed in a bin. In how many ways can 3 balls be drawn, in order, from the bin, if:
  1. each ball remains outside the bin after it is drawn?
  2. each ball is placed back into the bin immediately after it is drawn?
  3. the first ball is replaced after it is drawn but the second ball remains outside the bin?
1.5.4★
Can you simplify P(n, k)P(n − k, j) where n, k and j are integers such that k ≤ n and j ≤ n − k? Hints: 94
Answers / SolutionsAnswer p. 3–4
1.5.1
  1. P(8, 3) = 8! / (8 − 3)! = 8! / 5! = 8 × 7 × 6 = 336.
  2. P(20, 4) = 20! / (20 − 4)! = 20! / 16! = 20 × 19 × 18 × 17 = 116,280.
  3. P(30, 1) = 30! / (30 − 1)! = 30! / 29! = 30.
  4. P(6, 5) = 6! / (6 − 5)! = 6! / 1! = 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 720.
  5. P(50, 3) = 50! / (50 − 3)! = 50! / 47! = 50 × 49 × 48 = 117,600.
1.5.2
P(n, n) = n! / (n − n)! = n! / 0! = n!.
This is the number of permutations of size n from a group of n objects, or just the number of ways to order n objects.
1.5.3
  1. There are 12 choices for the first ball, 11 remaining choices for the second ball, and 10 remaining choices for the third ball, for a total of 12 × 11 × 10 = 1320 possible drawings. Note that this is also just P(12, 3).
  2. There are 12 options for each ball to be drawn, so there are a total of 123 = 1728 possible drawings.
  3. There are 12 options for the first ball, 12 options for the second ball since it is replaced, and 11 options for the third ball since the second ball is not replaced, for a total of 12 × 12 × 11 = 1584 possible drawings.
1.5.4

Using algebra, we see that

P(n, k)P(n − k, j) = n! / (n − k)! × (n − k)! / (n − k − j)!
= n! / (n − k − j)! = P(n, k + j).

We are counting the number of permutations of size k from a group of n objects and then multiplying by the number of permutations of size j from the remaining n − k objects. If we combine these two steps, it is the same as the number of permutations of size k + j from the original n objects.
