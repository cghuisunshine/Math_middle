1.4.1
Suppose I have 6 shirts and 5 ties. How many shirt-and-tie outfits can I make?
1.4.2
For each of 8 colors, I have one shirt and one tie of that color. How many shirt-and-tie outfits can I make if I refuse to wear a shirt and a tie of the same color?
1.4.3
How many license plates consist of 2 letters followed by 3 numbers?
1.4.4
How many license plates consist of 3 letters, followed by 2 even digits, followed by 2 odd digits?
1.4.5
In how many ways can I stack 5 books on a shelf?
1.4.6
Suppose that I have 6 different books, 2 of which are math books. In how many ways can I stack my 6 books on a shelf if I want a math book on both ends of the stack? Hints: 1
1.4.7
There are 8 sprinters in the Olympic 100-meter finals. The gold medal goes to first place, silver to second, and bronze to third. In how many ways can the medals be awarded?
1.4.8
My club has 15 members. In how many ways can we choose a president, vice-president, secretary, and treasurer, if no member can hold more than one office?
1.4.9
Compute each of the following without using a calculator:
  1. 9! / 8!
  2. 42! / 40!
  3. 8! − 7! Hints: 201
Answers / SolutionsAnswer p. 2–3
1.4.1
There are 6 choices of shirt and 5 choices of tie, so the total number of outfits is 6 × 5 = 30.
1.4.2
There are 8 options for the shirt, and only 7 options for the tie because one of the ties has the same color as the shirt, so the number of outfits is 8 × 7 = 56.
1.4.3
There are 26 choices of letters for each of the first two spots, and 10 choices of digits for each of the next 3 spots, for a total of 262 × 103 = 676,000 different plates.
1.4.4
There are 26 choices of letters for each of the first three spots, and 5 choices of digits for each of the last four spots: 5 even digit choices and 5 odd digit choices. This gives a total of 263 × 54 = 10,985,000.
1.4.5
There are 5 options for the bottom book, 4 remaining options for the next book, 3 remaining options for the next book, 2 remaining options for the fourth book, and finally only 1 option for the top book. This gives a total of 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120 ways to stack 5 books.
1.4.6
We first place the math books. We have two choices for the bottom book, and then the only one remaining choice for the top book is the other math book. Then we place the four other books in the middle. There are 4 choices for the first book, 3 choices for the second, 2 choices for the third, and only 1 choice for the fourth. So the total number of ways the books can be placed is 2 × 1 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 48.
1.4.7
There are 8 possible sprinters to award the gold to, then 7 remaining sprinters for the silver, and then 6 left for the bronze, for a total of 8 × 7 × 6 = 336 ways to award the medals.
1.4.8
There are 15 choices for president, 14 choices for vice-president, 13 choices for secretary, and 12 choices for treasurer, for a total of 15 × 14 × 13 × 12 = 32,760 different choices.
1.4.9
  1. 9! / 8! = (9 × 8 × 7 × 6 × ··· × 1) / (8 × 7 × 6 × ··· × 1) = 9.
  2. 42! / 40! = (42 × 41 × 40 × 39 × ··· × 1) / (40 × 39 × ··· × 1) = 42 × 41 = 1,722.
  3. 8! − 7! = 8 × 7! − 7! = 7!(8 − 1) = 7! × 7 = 5040 × 7 = 35,280.
