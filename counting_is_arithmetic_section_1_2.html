Answers / Solutions Answer p. 1

1.2.1

We subtract 35 from each member of the list to get 1, 2, 3, . . . , 57, 58, so there are 58 numbers. Note that this follows the b − a + 1 formula for how many numbers there are between a and b inclusive, as 93 − 36 + 1 = 58.

1.2.2

Dividing each member of the list by 2, we get 2, 3, 4, . . . , 64, 65, and then subtracting 1, we get 1, 2, 3, . . . , 63, 64, so there are 64 numbers.

1.2.3

Add 3 to each member of the list to get −30, −25, −20, . . . , 55, 60, and divide by 5 to get −6, −5, −4, . . . , 11, 12. Using the inclusive integer formula, we get 12 − (−6) + 1 = 19, so there are 19 numbers. We could also add 7 to each number in the list to get 1, 2, 3, . . . , 18, 19, and see that there are 19 numbers.

1.2.4

First, we reverse the list to become 39, 42, . . . , 144, 147. Then we divide each number by 3 to get 13, 14, . . . , 48, 49, so there are 49 − 13 + 1 = 37 numbers.

1.2.5

Multiplying each number by 3, we get 11, 13, 15, . . . , 79, 81. Then we can subtract 1 and divide by 2 to get 5, 6, 7, . . . , 39, 40. So there are 40 − 5 + 1 = 36 numbers.

1.2.6

7 × 21 = 147 < 150 < 154 = 7 × 22, so 21 positive multiples of 7 are less than 150.

1.2.7

Since 7² < 50 < 8² and 15² < 250 < 16², the squares between 50 and 250 are 8², 9², 10², . . . , 15². So there are 15 − 8 + 1 = 8 such squares.

1.2.8

Since 1² < 5 < 3² and 13² < 211 < 15², we have the list 3², 5², 7², . . . , 13², which has the same number of elements as 3, 5, 7, . . . , 13, which has 6 elements.

1.2.9*

Note that 17⁴ = 83,521 < 100,000 < 104,976 = 18⁴. Since 17.5⁴ ≈ 16 × 17 × 18 × 19, we check the value of 16 × 17 × 18 × 19 = 93,024. Also 17 × 18 × 19 × 20 = 116,280, so 16 × 17 × 18 × 19 is the largest product of four consecutive positive integers which is less than 100,000. So there are 16 sets.
