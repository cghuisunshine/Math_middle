1.3.1Question

Let the number of red 4-door cars be x. Since there are 12 red cars and 15 4-door cars, the number of red 2-door cars is 12 − x, while the number of white 4-door cars is 15 − x.

All four categories must add to the total number of cars:

x + (12 − x) + (15 − x) + 4 = 20, so x = 11.

Venn diagram
Answer: 11 cars are both 4-door and red.
1.3.2Question

There are 12 players total and 7 are taking biology, so 12 − 7 = 5 players are not taking biology.

Since every player is taking at least one of biology or chemistry, those 5 players must be taking chemistry alone. In addition, 2 players are taking both sciences.

Therefore, the number taking chemistry is 5 + 2 = 7.

Answer: 7 players are taking chemistry.
1.3.3Question

Let the number of blue-eyed students be x. Then the number of blond students is 2x.

Since 6 students are both blue-eyed and blond, the blue-eyed non-blond group is x − 6, and the blond non-blue-eyed group is 2x − 6. There are 3 students in neither group.

So the class total gives

(x − 6) + (2x − 6) + 6 + 3 = 30, so x = 11.

Venn diagram
Answer: 11 students have blue eyes.
1.3.4*Question

Start in the center of a three-circle Venn diagram. There are 9 dogs that can do all three tricks.

For exactly two tricks:

sit ∩ roll only = 18 − 9 = 9

stay ∩ roll only = 12 − 9 = 3

sit ∩ stay only = 17 − 9 = 8

Then find the “only one trick” regions:

sit only = 50 − 9 − 8 − 9 = 24

stay only = 29 − 3 − 8 − 9 = 9

roll only = 34 − 9 − 3 − 9 = 13

Venn diagram

Include the 9 dogs that do no tricks. The total number of dogs is

9 + 9 + 3 + 8 + 24 + 13 + 9 + 9 = 84.

The number that can do exactly two tricks is

8 + 9 + 3 = 20.

Answers: (a) 84 dogs. (b) 20 dogs can do exactly 2 tricks.
1.3.5*Question

Since x people are in French and z are in both classes, x − z students are only in French.

Similarly, since y people are in Spanish and z are in both classes, y − z students are only in Spanish.

Everyone is in exactly one of these three regions: French only, Spanish only, or both. So the total is

(x − z) + (y − z) + z = x + y − z.

Answer: x + y − z.
