1.17
We can add 0.5 to each member of the list, to make it easier to deal with:
3, 6, 9, 12, . . . , 81, 84.
Now if we divide by 3, we get
1, 2, 3, 4, . . . , 27, 28,
so there are 28 numbers in the list.
1.18
Subtract 2 from the list to get 4, 8, 12, . . . , 80, 84, and then divide by 4 to get 1, 2, 3, . . . , 20, 21. So the list has 21 numbers.
1.19
Note that 7 × 14 = 98 < 100 < 105 = 7 × 15 and 7 × 142 = 994 < 1000 < 1001 = 7 × 143. So the list of 3-digit numbers divisible by 7 is 105, 112, . . . , 994, and when we divide this list by 7, we get 15, 16, 17, . . . , 141, 142, which has 142 − 15 + 1 = 128 numbers.
1.20
Let the number of lefty jazz lovers be x. Then 8 − x lefties dislike jazz and 15 − x jazz lovers are righties. Since the number of righty jazz dislikers is 2 and the total number of club members is 20, we can add these four exclusive categories:
x + (8 − x) + (15 − x) + 2 = 20, so x = 5.
Therefore, 5 club members are left-handed and like jazz music.
1.21
We can solve this using basic arithmetic: If 13 of the students with calculators are girls, and 22 students total have calculators, then 22 − 13 = 9 of the students with calculators are boys. So if 9 boys have calculators, and there are 16 boys total, then 16 − 9 = 7 boys don’t have calculators.
Answer: 7.

Alternatively, we could solve this using a Venn diagram. Note that we do not have enough information to determine how many girls didn’t bring their calculators.

1.22
There are 5 options for shirts, 6 options for pants, and 8 options for hats, for a total of 5 × 6 × 8 = 240 outfits.
1.23
There are 26 options for the first letter, only 5 options for the second letter because it must be a vowel, and 25 options for the third letter because it can be any letter except the first. This gives 26 × 5 × 25 = 3,250 combinations.
1.24
We must count the number of permutations of 6 people. There are 6 choices for the first person in line, 5 choices for the second person in line, and so on. So the answer is 6! = 720.
1.25
There are 7 options for the first hat, 6 options for the second hat, and so on. So the answer is 7! = 5,040.
1.26
5 players are being chosen in order out of 12, so the answer is P(12, 5) = 12! / 7! = 12 × 11 × 10 × 9 × 8 = 95,040.
1.27
  1. Obviously the only arrangement is T. There is 1 arrangement.
  2. There are two arrangements: IT and TI. One starts with I and one starts with T, for a total of 2.
  3. There are 6 arrangements of ETI: EIT, ETI, IET, ITE, TEI, TIE. Two of them start with E; similarly, two start with T and two start with I.
  4. There are 4! = 24 arrangements of SETI:
    EIST, EITS, ESIT, ESTI, ETIS, ETSI, IEST, IETS, ISET, ISTE, ITES, ITSE, SEIT, SETI, SIET, SITE, STEI, STIE, TEIS, TESI, TIES, TISE, TSEI, TSIE
    The 6 arrangements that start with S are SEIT, SETI, SIET, SITE, STEI, STIE. The last 3 letters of each of these are exactly the 6 arrangements of ETI from part (c).
  5. We can think of this as 5 choices for the first letter, then 4! = 24 arrangements for the other four letters, for a total of 5 × 4! = 5! = 120 arrangements.
