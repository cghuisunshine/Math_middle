Teaching Script: Fencing Club Problem

Problem: In a fencing club, every student is either in middle school or high school, and each student competes in either épée or sabre. There are 72 students in total. How many are middle school épée competitors?

This page includes the original question, then gives a step-by-step tutor script. Each step quotes the exact sentence from the problem that justifies the calculation.

Core strategy: define a variable Use relationship clues Build a table Solve 9x = 72 Final answer: 8

Step list

Original Question 1. Read and organize the categories 2. Choose the unknown 3. Use the épée clue 4. Use the middle-school sabre clue 5. Use the sabre half-in-high-school clue 6. Fill in the table 7. Use the total of 72 8. Solve for x 9. State the final answer

Original Question

1.32* In my fencing club, all the participants are in middle school or high school, and each participant competes in épée or sabre. There are twice as many épée competitors who are in high school as there are épée competitors in middle school. Of the students in the club who are in middle school, there are three times as many sabre competitors as there are épée competitors. Half of the sabre competitors who are in the club are in high school. If there are 72 students in my fencing club, how many are middle school épée competitors?

Quick structure

We let x be the number of middle school épée competitors. Then the other groups can all be written in terms of x. After that, the total number of students gives an equation.

Understand the setup

Read the problem and sort the categories

#01

Related sentence from the problem

“all the participants are in middle school or high school, and each participant competes in épée or sabre.”

Tutor says

Before calculating, let’s sort the information. Every participant is classified in two ways: by school level, which is middle school or high school, and by weapon, which is épée or sabre. That means we are working with a two-by-two table.

Board / Image cue

Draw a 2×2 table: rows for middle school and high school, columns for épée and sabre.

Choose a variable

Let the target group be x

#02

Related sentence from the problem

“how many are middle school épée competitors?”

Tutor says

The problem asks for the number of middle school épée competitors, so that is the smartest group to call x. Once we choose that, we can express all the other groups using the clues in the problem.

Board / Image cue

Write: x = ext{ middle school épée competitors}.

First relationship

Use the high school épée clue

#03

Related sentence from the problem

“There are twice as many épée competitors who are in high school as there are épée competitors in middle school.”

Tutor says

The problem says there are twice as many high school épée competitors as middle school épée competitors. Since middle school épée is x, high school épée must be 2x.

Board / Image cue

Write: HS épée = 2x.

Second relationship

Use the middle school sabre clue

#04

Related sentence from the problem

“Of the students in the club who are in middle school, there are three times as many sabre competitors as there are épée competitors.”

Tutor says

Next, the problem says that among the middle school students, there are three times as many sabre competitors as épée competitors. Since middle school épée is x, middle school sabre is 3x.

Board / Image cue

Write: MS sabre = 3x.

Third relationship

Use the clue about all sabre competitors

#05

Related sentence from the problem

“Half of the sabre competitors who are in the club are in high school.”

Tutor says

The problem also says that half of the sabre competitors are in high school. That means the other half of the sabre competitors are in middle school. We already know middle school sabre is 3x, so high school sabre must also be 3x.

Board / Image cue

Write: HS sabre = 3x.

Tutor tip

This is a good place to pause and ask the student: “If half are in high school, what fraction are in middle school?”

Organize everything

Fill in the full table

#06

Related sentence from the problem

“all the participants are in middle school or high school, and each participant competes in épée or sabre.”
“There are twice as many épée competitors who are in high school as there are épée competitors in middle school.”
“Of the students in the club who are in middle school, there are three times as many sabre competitors as there are épée competitors.”
“Half of the sabre competitors who are in the club are in high school.”

Tutor says

Now we fill in the table. Middle school has x épée and 3x sabre, so its row total is 4x. High school has 2x épée and 3x sabre, so its row total is 5x. Then the grand total is 4x + 5x = 9x.

Board / Image cue

Complete the table and show the row totals and grand total.

Use the total

Set up the equation

#07

Related sentence from the problem

“If there are 72 students in my fencing club...”

Tutor says

The problem says there are 72 students altogether. Our table says the total number of students is 9x. So we can now write the equation 9x = 72.

Board / Image cue

Write: 9x = 72.

Solve

Find x

#08

Related sentence from the problem

“If there are 72 students in my fencing club...”

Tutor says

Now solve the equation. Divide both sides by 9. That gives x = 72 ÷ 9 = 8.

Board / Image cue

Show: x = 8.

Answer the question

State the final answer clearly

#09

Related sentence from the problem

“how many are middle school épée competitors?”

Tutor says

Finally, always return to the meaning of the variable. We defined x to be the number of middle school épée competitors. Since x = 8, the answer is 8 middle school épée competitors.

Board / Image cue

Final answer: 8.
