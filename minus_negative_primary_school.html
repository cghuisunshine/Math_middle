Why Does Minus a Negative Mean Add?

A primary-school friendly explanation using debts, cards, and a number line

1. The Big Idea

First, remember two simple meanings:

Minus means take away.
Negative means below zero, or owing something.
5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8

When we subtract a negative number, we are taking away something bad. That makes the answer bigger.

2. Story: Removing a Debt

Imagine you have $5.

A negative number is like a debt.

−3 means: “I owe $3.”

Now look at this:

5 − (−3)
This means: “I have $5, and someone removes my $3 debt.”

If someone removes your $3 debt, you become $3 richer.

5 − (−3) = 8

3. Card Model

Use two kinds of cards:

+
−

+ card means one dollar you have.

− card means one dollar you owe.

Start with five positive cards:

+
+
+
+
+

That is 5.

Subtracting −3 means taking away three debt cards. Taking away debt helps you, so the number goes up by 3.

5 − (−3) = 5 + 3

4. Number Line Explanation

For normal subtraction, we move left. For example, 5 − 3 means move left 3 steps.

But subtracting a negative means move the opposite way. So 5 − (−3) means move right 3 steps.

5. Easy Sentence to Remember

“Subtracting a negative means taking away something bad, so the number gets bigger.”
a − (−b) = a + b

6. Try It

Question: What is 7 − (−2)?

7 − (−2) = 7 + 2 = 9. Taking away a debt of 2 makes the number 2 bigger.
