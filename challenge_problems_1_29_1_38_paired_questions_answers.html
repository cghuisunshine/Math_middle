1.30Answer
Let x be the number of people wearing all three items. Then:

30 − x wear just bathing suits and sunglasses, 25 − x wear just bathing suits and hats, and 40 − x wear just sunglasses and hats.

Just sunglasses: 110 − (30 − x) − (40 − x) − x = 40 + x.

Similarly, just hats is 30 + x, and just bathing suits is 15 + x.

Since all 190 people wear at least one item,

190 = (15 + x) + (40 + x) + (30 + x) + (25 − x) + (30 − x) + (40 − x) + x = 180 + x. So x = 10. Therefore, 10 people are wearing all three items.
1.31Answer
Let x be the number of students taking physics, so the number taking chemistry is 2x. There are 15 students taking all three.

Because 30 take both physics and calculus, the “physics and calculus only” region is 30 − 15 = 15. Similarly, the “calculus and chemistry only” region is 75 − 15 = 60, and the “physics and chemistry only” region is also 75 − 15 = 60.

Calculus only is 180 − 60 − 15 − 15 = 90. Physics only is x − 90. Chemistry only is 2x − 135.

(x − 90) + (2x − 135) + 90 + 60 + 15 + 60 + 15 + 15 = 360. Solving gives x = 110. So 110 students take physics.
1.32★Answer
Let the number of middle school boys be x. Then high school boys are 2x. Since there are three times as many middle school girls as middle school boys, middle school girls are 3x. Since this represents half the girls in the club, high school girls are also 3x. 72 = x + 2x + 3x + 3x = 9x. Thus x = 8. There are 8 middle school boys.

1.34★Answer
The units digit of 1! is 1, of 2! is 2, of 3! is 6, and of 4! = 24 is 4. For all n ≥ 5, n! is a multiple of 10, so its units digit is 0. Therefore the units digit of the whole sum is the units digit of 1 + 2 + 6 + 4 = 13, so the answer is 3.
1.35Answer
For n! to be divisible by 9, it must contain two factors of 3. The first factorial with two factors of 3 is 6!, since 6! = 6 · 5 · 4 · 3 · 2 · 1. Since 9 divides 6!, every factorial from 6! through 100! is divisible by 9. 100 − 6 + 1 = 95.
1.36Answer
Since 1/n > 3/100, n must be positive. Multiplying the inequality by 100n gives: 50n > 100 > 3n. From 50n > 100, we get n > 2. From 100 > 3n, we get n < 100/3. Therefore the integers are 3, 4, 5, …, 32, 33. There are 33 − 3 + 1 = 31 such integers.
1.37Answer
  1. Each row has odd-numbered chairs 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, for 6 odd-numbered chairs in each row. Since there are 11 rows, there are 11 × 6 = 66 chairs with odd numbers.
  2. Separate into two cases. If n is odd, each row has (n + 1)/2 odd-numbered chairs and there are n rows, for a total of n(n + 1)/2. If n is even, each row has n/2 odd-numbered chairs and there are n rows, for a total of n2/2. Equivalently, the total is n⌈n/2⌉.
1.38Answer
There are 21 rows of dots and 11 columns of dots. Since there are 20 vertical toothpicks in each column and 11 columns, there are 20 × 11 = 220 vertical toothpicks. Since there are 10 horizontal toothpicks in each row and 21 rows, there are 21 × 10 = 210 horizontal toothpicks. Therefore the total is 220 + 210 = 430 toothpicks.
