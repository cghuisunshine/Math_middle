Prime Factorization and the Least Common Multiple

A standalone study page based on OpenStax Prealgebra 2e, Section 2.5. Learn how to break composite numbers into prime factors and use those factors to find the least common multiple.

Prime factorization Factor tree Ladder method LCM

Learning Objectives

  • Find the prime factorization of a composite number.
  • Find the least common multiple, or LCM, of two numbers.
  • Use prime factorization to make LCM questions easier.

1. Key Vocabulary

Prime Number

A number greater than 1 with exactly two factors: 1 and itself.

2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, ...

Composite Number

A number greater than 1 with more than two factors.

12 = 1 × 12 = 2 × 6 = 3 × 4

Prime Factorization

Writing a number as a product of prime numbers.

36 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 = 2² × 3²
Definition: The prime factorization of a number is the product of prime numbers that equals the number.

2. Factor Tree Method

The factor tree method breaks a composite number into factor pairs until every branch ends in a prime number.

Steps

  1. Choose any factor pair of the given number.
  2. If a factor is prime, stop that branch.
  3. If a factor is composite, factor it again.
  4. Continue until all branches end in prime numbers.
  5. Multiply the prime numbers to write the prime factorization.

Example A: Prime factorization of 48

Start with one factor pair:

48 = 2 × 24

Then factor 24:

24 = 4 × 6

Then factor 4 and 6:

4 = 2 × 2,    6 = 2 × 3

Collect all prime factors:

48 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 = 2⁴ × 3

Example B: Prime factorization of 84

84 = 4 × 21
4 = 2 × 2,    21 = 3 × 7
84 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 7 = 2² × 3 × 7
Important: You can start with different factor pairs, but the final prime factorization will be the same. For example, 48 could start as 2 × 24 or 6 × 8, but both lead to 2⁴ × 3.

3. Ladder Method

The ladder method, also called stacked division, repeatedly divides a number by prime numbers.

Steps

  1. Divide the number by the smallest prime that divides evenly.
  2. Keep dividing by that prime until it no longer divides evenly.
  3. Move to the next prime number.
  4. Continue until the quotient is prime.
  5. The prime factors are the divisors on the side plus the final prime quotient.

Example C: Prime factorization of 120

Divide byResult
2120 ÷ 2 = 60
260 ÷ 2 = 30
230 ÷ 2 = 15
315 ÷ 3 = 5

The final quotient 5 is prime, so stop.

120 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 5 = 2³ × 3 × 5

Example D: Prime factorization of 48 by ladder

Divide byResult
248 ÷ 2 = 24
224 ÷ 2 = 12
212 ÷ 2 = 6
26 ÷ 2 = 3
48 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 = 2⁴ × 3

4. Least Common Multiple by Listing Multiples

Common multiple: a number that is a multiple of both numbers.
Least common multiple: the smallest common multiple. It is often written as LCM.

Steps

  1. List several multiples of each number.
  2. Find the numbers that appear in both lists.
  3. Choose the smallest common number.

Example E: LCM of 12 and 18

Multiples of 12: 12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72, ...
Multiples of 18: 18, 36, 54, 72, 90, ...

The smallest common multiple is 36.

LCM(12, 18) = 36

5. Least Common Multiple by Prime Factors

This method is usually faster for larger numbers.

Steps

  1. Find the prime factorization of each number.
  2. For each prime, choose the highest power needed.
  3. Multiply those selected prime powers.
Simple rule: LCM uses the highest power of every prime that appears in either number.

Example F: LCM of 15 and 18

15 = 3 × 5
18 = 2 × 3²

Use the highest powers: 2, 3², and 5.

LCM(15, 18) = 2 × 3² × 5 = 90

Example G: LCM of 50 and 100

50 = 2 × 5²
100 = 2² × 5²

Use the highest powers: 2² and 5².

LCM(50, 100) = 2² × 5² = 100

Example H: LCM of 24 and 36

24 = 2³ × 3
36 = 2² × 3²

Use the highest power of 2 and the highest power of 3.

LCM(24, 36) = 2³ × 3² = 8 × 9 = 72

6. Practice Questions

Prime Factorization

  1. Find the prime factorization of 60.
  2. Find the prime factorization of 72.
  3. Find the prime factorization of 96.
  4. Find the prime factorization of 150.
  5. Find the prime factorization of 210.

LCM

  1. Find LCM(8, 12).
  2. Find LCM(10, 15).
  3. Find LCM(18, 24).
  4. Find LCM(20, 28).
  5. Find LCM(45, 60).
Show Answers
  1. 60 = 2² × 3 × 5
  2. 72 = 2³ × 3²
  3. 96 = 2⁵ × 3
  4. 150 = 2 × 3 × 5²
  5. 210 = 2 × 3 × 5 × 7
  6. LCM(8, 12) = 24
  7. LCM(10, 15) = 30
  8. LCM(18, 24) = 72
  9. LCM(20, 28) = 140
  10. LCM(45, 60) = 180

7. Student Shortcut

For LCM questions, split the work into small prime-power questions.

Example: LCM of 72 and 108

72 = 2³ × 3²
108 = 2² × 3³

Ask separately:

  • Highest power of 2? 2³
  • Highest power of 3? 3³
LCM(72, 108) = 2³ × 3³ = 8 × 27 = 216
